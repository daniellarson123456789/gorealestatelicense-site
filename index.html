The Florida Real Estate License Roadmap

Getting licensed is easier to understand when you break it into steps.

1

Start the required Florida licensing course

Florida sales associate applicants need approved pre-license education before sitting for the state exam. In Florida, that course is 63 hours. Aceable Agent is our recommended online starting point for students who want a modern, flexible course experience.

2

Submit your Florida license application

The course is not the only step. You also need to apply through the Florida Department of Business and Professional Regulation, usually called DBPR.

3

Complete fingerprints and background requirements

Florida real estate applicants generally need to complete fingerprinting/background requirements as part of the application process. Do not wait until the last minute. Processing can take time.

Application checklist
4

Prepare for the state exam

The required course helps you become eligible. Exam prep helps you get ready to pass. FastPass adds Florida-specific exam prep and instructor support before the state exam.

5

Schedule and pass the state exam

After your application is approved and your course completion is valid, you can schedule the Florida real estate sales associate exam through the state’s testing vendor.

6

Activate your license with a broker

Passing the exam does not automatically mean you are active and ready to work. To perform licensed real estate services in Florida, your license needs to be activated with a Florida real estate broker.

Florida real estate terms, explained simply

Quick definitions for the words students see before, during, and after enrollment.

Licensing course

The required Florida pre-license education course for sales associate applicants. In Florida, this course is 63 hours.

DBPR

The Florida Department of Business and Professional Regulation, which handles license applications.

State exam

The licensing exam you must pass before your license can move forward.

License activation

The process of placing your license with a Florida real estate broker so you can work.

Post-Licensing vs. CE

Your first renewal is different from later renewals. Florida sales associates generally complete post-licensing first, then regular continuing education in later renewal cycles.

Common Florida real estate license questions

Short answers for the questions people usually search before they start.

What course do I need to get a Florida real estate license?

Most new Florida sales associate applicants need a Florida-approved pre-license course before sitting for the state exam. In Florida, that course is 63 hours.

Can I take the Florida real estate course online?

Yes. Online options are available through approved course providers. This site recommends Aceable Agent for students who want a modern, flexible, self-paced online course experience.

Is the course the same as exam prep?

No. The course is the required education. Exam prep is additional study help designed to prepare you for the state exam. That is why the recommended path is Aceable Agent for the course, then FastPass if you want Florida-focused exam prep support.

Do I need to apply before taking the exam?

You need to meet the state’s application and approval requirements before scheduling or sitting for the state exam.

What happens after I pass?

You generally need to activate your license with a Florida real estate broker before performing licensed real estate services.

What is FastPass40?

FastPass40 is the code for $40 off FastPass exam prep. Regular price is $140. Final price with code: $100.

What is the difference between post-licensing and CE?

Post-licensing applies to your first renewal after initial licensure. Continuing education applies to later regular renewals.
